Projects

Habitual - mobile design

Overview

This is a project that we did in our Udemy course. We created our own mobile app for Habitual, the purpose was to create an interactive online shop where users can buy products, see what Habitual have to offer, sign up for monthly specials and stay updated with all the news or new products and to add products to their wishlists, to add interests and the main goal was to get users to the check out point and to close sales.
Other requests was that the application needed a fun, clean, and colourful look and feel, with attractive call to actions and it should be very user-friendly, navigation should be easy.

My motive for this case study was to take full ownership of the various roles involved in designing a product such as: User Researcher, User Experience Designer, Product Designer.

Understanding Habitual and its customers

I began my case study with a brand analysis of Habitual, learning about its competitors and customer demographics along the way.

Target Audience Demographics

Habitual’s target demographics is women ages 16–55 years old with high disposable income. Its customer base also includes men age 14–40 years old.

Duration: 3 months.
Tools: Figma, Invision, CorelDraw, Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator
Persona of a typical habitual user: I created a user persona to put a face on Habitual’s target user and visualize various aspects of their behavior and motivations. This persona was roughly based on the users interviewed during the usability testing.
Sketching and prototyping I scoped out Habitual’s competitors and other shopping apps and made observations on which layouts worked best. I sketched out possible solutions for the main screens before committing to high fidelity designs. This allowed me to prototype different layouts rather than focusing on design details.
User Research: I made a prototype of the app and asked a few users to perform a few predefined tasks as I recorded their actions and took notes. Some of the tasks/ questions were: 1) Find my recent purchases. 2) Try to save a product you like but don’t want to buy right away. 3) Navigate to my wishlist/”loves”.
This is what users had to say during the usability tests: “I know its on here…not sure where” “Uhh…don’t know” “How do I go back?”
Identify the problem: The following three issues stood out the most: Make app more user-friendly and easier to navigate, add colours and make call to actions/ buttons more visible.
UX Suggestions & Redesign: I recreated the userflow of the app to make everything more user-friendly, I have also changed the icons in the navigation bar to make navigation easier (the cart icon is now more visible as well as the wishlist icon), the colours now match the corporate identity and the buttons are more visible through out the app.
Validation Testing: To validate the changes I made, I surveyed users with my prototype. I asked the same questions from my initial tests and recorded their behavior and feedback. Here’s what people had to say about the redesign: “Did you make this app? This is great.” “I like this better, I can find my way through the app.” “seemed more obvious.”

The Print Place - web design

Overview

The project goal was to recreate the website for The Print Place. Make it lighter, brighter and fun. The site should be more interactive and create a small online shop to seal the deal while customers visit the site. Fix the contact form and add a map, link contact number, email and location, make call to actions more attractive and add more localized pictures to give it a personal touch. Design website with users / customers in mind.

“People ignore design that ignores people” — Frank Chimero

Duration: 2 months
Tools: Figma, Invision, CorelDraw, Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Priemere Pro, After Effects
Discuss project goals: Discuss the project with the client to determine the project goals and client values, I had to create an updated corporate identity for The Print Place for a guideline and to work on brand strategy.
Competitor Research: I did research on the brand and it's competition and based on what I found I created a persona of a client of The Print Place.
Sketching and wireframes: I kept in mind that the project goal was to create a more interactive website, I did some research and sketched a pretty interactive website with a few call to actions in between to encourage visitors to shop or contact the company.
Design Product: I designed the product based off the competitor research, sketches, client requirements and the client persona, I showed the client the layout and asked them for feedback on the design.
Implement solutions: I created extra pages, the gallery and shop, I made space for a video at the top of the home page and gallery page so that the client can showcase their products when users land on the page, the user can also switch through the tabs to watch videos on different products or services, this makes the site more interactive and fun. The Shop page will help close sales and incourage clients to contact the company.
Evaluate the work: Evaluate the work and improve it, I asked the company and their staff to test it and to give me feedback on how they find the website and if they find it to be more user-friendly, fun and more interactive than before.
As a result of the redesign, the success rate for tasks increased to 100%.
